北京オリンピックシンボルデザイン
beijing olympic symbol design

I entered the symbol design competition for the 2008 Summer Olympic Games. The design introduced here received an honorable mention. With the idea that the charm of the manmade physical environment of traditional China lies in embellishment, I strove to design a symbol that went in a direction that was not devoted to simplicity. Based on the motif of Chinese hieroglyphs, each portion is a pictogram of an athlete in the games. The integration of the pictograms representing a variety of games creates a symbol that is complex, yet centripetal. The individual pictograms are designed to move as motion graphics.

Symbol Design
2003
AD: Kenya Hara
D: Kenya Hara, Kayoko Takeo,Yukie Inoue, Satoko Hinomizu
Coordinator: New Media of Japan and China Co.,Ltd
CL: The Beijing Organizing Committee for the Games of the XXIX Olympiad

北京オリンピックシンボルデザイン
beijing olympic symbol design

In 2000, Matsuya Ginza shifted its central image from lifestyle design to fashion. It installed major international fashion brands, with a Louis Vuitton brand shop on the corner. In order to accommodate all of the brands, it needed to overhaul its identity.

The posters announcing the renewal were made with embroidery. A computer-controlled sewing machine was used in place of a printing press. Here, also we stressed communication weighted with a sense of touch. This is a reflection of the attitude of a department store that offers an experience one can savor with all five senses.

VI System, Advertising
2000
AD: Kenya Hara
D: Kenya Hara, Chihiro Murakami, Maho Ike, Yuji Koiso, Satoko Hinomizu
I: Yumeko Kato (poster)

北京オリンピックシンボルデザイン
beijing olympic symbol design

In the HIROSHIMA APPEALS project, initiated by Yusaku Kamekura, one designer is assigned to pass on the legacy each year by creating a poster. Kenya Hara designed the 2017 HIROSHIMA APPEALS poster. In this work, called the Mushroom Illustration, Kenya Hara's thoughts and prayers for HIROSHIMA are illustrated in a mushroom cloud seen from directly below. Rough sketches can be seen below. One shows a view looking up at the cloud from below, while another looks down upon the earth from space. The final version was achieved by cropping the original drawing.
