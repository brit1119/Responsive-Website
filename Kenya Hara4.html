Nippon no Design, which was published in 2011, provided concrete proposals for a vision for Japan's future, and was selected for the Japan Publishing Industry Foundation for Culture's Japan Library series, a project of the Cabinet Office. It was published in English as DESIGNING JAPAN.

100白
100 Whites

Japanese | English

Following White, which was first published in 2008 by Chuokoron-Shinsha, 100 Whites by Kenya Hara deals with the theme of whiteness, a concept closely related to his design philosophy. It brings together in one volume a hundred installments of a serial essay the author wrote for Yomiuri Online. While his previous book treated "white" as a concept, in 100 Whites, the author addresses specific phenomena of whiteness.

2018

AD: Kenya Hara
D: Kenya Hara, Tomoko Nishi

元形成
Ex-formation

Japanese | English

This is a record of teaching compiled over ten years in the Department of Science of Design at Musashino Art University, where Kenya Hara is the department head. "Ex-formation" refers not to "making things known", but rather to "making things unknown." The book introduces a communication design method that is based on the new concept of determining how to make people aware of what they do not know.

2017

AD: Kenya Hara
D: Kenya Hara, Tomoko Nishi

白
WHITE

Bilingual | Chinese | German | Korean

White is a work of Kenya Hara’s in which he tries to reassess white not as a color but as the foundation of Japanese sensibilities. This is a test, from the standpoint of design, of the study that runs through the aesthetics of books like novelist Junichiro Tanizaki’s In Praise of Shadows, philosopher Shuzo Kuki’s The Structure of Iki, art thinker Tenshin Okakura’s Book of Tea and agriculturalist Inazo Mitobe’s Bushido: The Soul of Japan.

2008

AD:Kenya Hara
PH:Yoshihiko Ueda,Yasuhiro Ishimoto
D:Kenya Hara

デザインの設計
DESINGING DESIGN

Japanese | English | Chinese

Lars Müller Publishers is well known among designers and architects for its intelligent editing. When this book was being made, the owner/designer Lars Müller told Kenya Hara, “Monographs ended ten years ago. If you’re going to put out a design book, you must be the author.” In the beginning, I envisioned a larger book than it turned out to be, but his advice changed my mind and the format was decided. This is not a monograph; it's a designer’s book.

2007

AD:Kenya Hara
D:Kenya Hara.Kaoru Matsuno
